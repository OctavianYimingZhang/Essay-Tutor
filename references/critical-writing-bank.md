# Critical Writing Bank

Use this reference when a task needs stronger critical stance, discussion, evaluation of sources, study limitations, theory limitations, method limitations, or constructive suggestions.

Use the language as reusable academic patterns. Adapt the sentence to the topic, identify the evidence problem, attach source support, interpret the limitation, and link back to the essay claim.

## Critical Stance Workflow

1. Identify the evidence problem: scope, method, construct, sample, time period, measurement, causal inference, inconsistency, or explanatory gap.
2. Choose the critical move: inadequacy of the field, weakness in a single study, theory limit, method limit, named-author criticism, or constructive suggestion.
3. Attach source support near the critical claim.
4. Explain why the limitation matters for the argument.
5. Link the evaluation back to the thesis, research question, or next paragraph.

## Identifying A Weakness In A Single Study Or Paper

- However, Smith fails to fully define what ...
- Jones fails to acknowledge the significance of ...
- The author overlooks the fact that X contributes to Y.
- What Smith fails to do is to draw a distinction between ...
- The paper would appear to be over-ambitious in its claims.
- Another weakness is that we are given no explanation of how ...
- No attempt was made to quantify the association between X and Y.
- The main weakness of the study is the failure to address how ...
- The study fails to consider the differing categories of damage that ...
- The research does not take into account pre-existing ... such as ...
- The author offers no explanation for the distinction between X and Y.
- Smith makes no attempt to differentiate between different types of X.

```text
However,
this method of analysis has a number of limitations.

```


## Using Evaluative Adjectives To Comment On Research

Reusable construction:

```text
In her / In their / In this
useful / timely / seminal / detailed / thorough / excellent / influential / important / innovative / pioneering / impressive / wide-ranging / comprehensive / ground-breaking
study (of X), / survey (of X), / analysis (of X), / examination (of X), / investigation (into X),

```

Use evaluative adjectives when they identify the research role. Support the evaluation with the study's contribution, method, influence, or limitation.
